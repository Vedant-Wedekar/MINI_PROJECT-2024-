Krsna's Teachings for Humanity

Dharma is the eternal law that governs the universe. Krishna teaches us to follow our dharma, our righteous duty, with sincerity and devotion. By adhering to dharma, we maintain balance in society and in our own lives.

Karma yoga is the path of selfless action. Krishna instructs us to perform our duties without attachment to the fruits of our actions. This leads to inner purification and spiritual growth, ultimately freeing us from the cycle of birth and death.

Bhakti yoga is the path of devotional service to the Divine. Krishna emphasizes that through loving devotion, we can transcend all material limitations and achieve the highest spiritual realization. Bhakti is accessible to all, regardless of social status or intellectual capacity.

Krsna's Divine Attributes

Krishna's flute symbolizes the divine call that awakens the soul. Its melodious sound represents the harmony of creation and the allure of spiritual enlightenment.

The peacock feather adorning Krishna's crown represents beauty, grace, and the cycle of time. It symbolizes the transient nature of worldly pleasures and the eternal beauty of the divine.

The celestial disc weapon of Krishna, Sudarshana Chakra, symbolizes the wheel of time and cosmic order. It represents divine protection and the destruction of ignorance.
